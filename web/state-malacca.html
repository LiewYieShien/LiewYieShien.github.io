Top things to do

Jonker Street

Located in the heart of Melaka's UNESCO World Heritage Site, is famed for its lively atmosphere, rich history, and cultural charm.

  • Stroll through the vibrant night market to discover unique souvenirs, traditional crafts, and delicious local cuisine.
  • Along the streets, explore artisanal shops, admire charming vintage Baba-Nyonya architecture.
  • Savor iconic street food like fragrant chicken rice ball, rich Nyonya laksa, Gula Melaka-infused Cendol, and indulge in treats from diverse food stalls.

Baba & Nyonya Heritage Museum

That showcases the local history of descendants of ethnic Chinese-Malays called Baba-Nyonya in the state. The museum was established in 1986 by Chan Kim Lay

  • Explore the rich culture of the Baba-Nyonya community by viewing antiques, traditional furniture, and beautifully crafted clothing
  • Join a guided tour to learn more about the history and significance of the exhibits.

Malacca Straits Mosque

Malacca Straits Mosque is a stunning mosque built on stilts over the sea, offering a blend of modern and traditional Islamic architecture. Located by the Malacca Strait,

  • Capture the mosque against the backdrop of the sea and sky, making for perfect photo opportunities.
  • Take in panoramic views of the Malacca Strait, especially beautiful at sunset.
  • Experience the peaceful atmosphere during prayer times if you're interested in the religious practices.

Christ Church, Malacca

Christ Church, Malacca, is an iconic 18th-century Dutch colonial church known for its striking red exterior and unique architectural features.

  • Located in Dutch Square, it is the oldest functioning Protestant church in Malaysia and a symbol of Melaka's rich colonial heritage.
  • Admire the unique red walls of the church, learn about its history.
  • Take iconic travel photos in front of the church, capturing its unique red architecture and rich historical ambiance.
